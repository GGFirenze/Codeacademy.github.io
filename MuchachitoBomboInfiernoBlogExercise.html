Jairo Perera: un rumbero New Orleans style de Santa Coloma de Gramenet (Bcn)


Jairo Perera Viedma, best known as Muchachito, is a guitarist, singer, and author of dozens of songs. Some of his most loved successes are Siempre que tú quieras, Eima, Luna, Tiempos Modernos among others. Jairo just closed his 2022 tour around Spain and is currently working on releasing his new Album. His previous albums were Vamos que nos vamos, Visto lo visto, Idas y vueltas, with a Maqueta released right before publishing El Jiro, a disc that contained música ochentera as the singer himself stated (music inspired by the 80's). Muchachito is about to launch a new album, but before doing so he published a vinile containing an adelanto(anticipation) of his next album. This disc contains two of his new successes: El club del paro (which was used as the soundtrack of the homonimous movie) and Demasiada agua y no hay piscina.

En mi Cabeza.

This song is actually a version of Kevin Johanssen's piece. In this remake, Jairo proposes an upbeat Rumba-inspired version


Tiempos Modernos.

This is one my all-time favourite songs ever written by Jairo. The song is about the absurdity of our current society. In this hit, Muchachito starts off saying how those who misbehave always receive the best treatment as opposed to honest individuals who often are given a hard time if they make the minor of mistakes. It is also a song that describes the cinicism of our times. Especially when it says "no te encariñes, vuélvete práctico", meaning don't soften up, be pragmatic.


Siempre Que Tú Quieras.

Razmatazz, Barcelona, 2011. This song could not miss from our recommended list. This is arguably an all-time favourite masterpiece by the colomense artist. The reason why I recommend this performance is rooted in the gradual development of the atmosphere around this performance. Jairo starts soft with a soave introduction to then break the theatre's walls under the rythm of his guitar. Siempre Que Tú Quieras is arguably the most replicated of his songs. One of the most known versions is the one from the group No Me Pises Que Llevo Chanclas. Jairo himself in a interview at Andreu Buenafuente's TV show Late Motiv stated that he came across some music bands in South America who claimed this song as their making. He also joked about him making fun of his same song during concerts. "Sometimes I change the lyrics and say Ojalá no te hubiera conocido en Murcia instead.


Vino y se Fue - Vilafranca Concert.

In this videoclip Jairo is in his One Man Band version that made his famous. As a matter of fact, Muchachito's formation as an artist happened for the most part as a street performer. This gave him the acute capability of merging himself with his audience. As he stated in previous interviews, performing in the street gave him the chance to see people reacting to his music right away. He often declared of having played in the streets of Paris because artists are better treated there in terms of permits to play and such. While playing there, he learned a big deal from other performers (from Jamaica, among others) who gave him some precious tips on how to make the public dance. In this occasion, he is with his lifelong friends and co-performer Santos de Veracruz, the painter who has been collaborating with Jairo since high school. We recommend that you watch the entire series of these videos shot in Vilafranca. There are 6 in total. Here they were in the casino of Vilafranca, in 2011


Muchachito Confinado - An Online Concert

Muchachito's love for his audience, the cravings for being surrounded by buena gente shines through. During lockdown, after a little while far from the stage, he decides to host a concert in the balcony of his brother's flat. Accompanied by his lifelong friend Santos de Veracruz via phone, Lagata Cristi from El Orfanato Eléctrico (house of production founded by himself) they bring Jairo into his fans' houses through means of an exciting YouTube live concert. In this case, there is not just a single song I recommend. I advise to listen to the entire trilogy of clips: Muchachito Confinado 3, 4, and 6 (do not worry about the numbers, the other videos have been removed by Muchachito's collaborators due to technical issues). Some of the songs he plays in this instance are Siempre Que Tú Quieras,La Viajera, and El Compadre.


Curiosities

Muchachito - Jairo Perera - was born in Santa Coloma de Gramenet. As he declared multiple times on TV he wanted to become guitarist and singer since he was 6. When he was 13 he created his first mini band. But it wouldn't be until 2000 when he would make his first album with his band Trimelón de Naranjus - band founded in 1993.

Not many people know that one his most known songs Siempre Que Tú Quieras was actually born in those early years. You can listen to its first version here. What would then become his longtime success was first named Sueños and had a more Swing essence compared to its late transformation into a mix of Rumba and Folk a lo desconocido. No wonder that Jairo often declared to be oftentimes 'slave' of this song, in that he has been singing it for 30 years. As Muchachito admits, though, this song cannot be removed from his repertory and, as a matter of fact, it is used to close his live performances under the screams of his affectionated public.

Some of his most loved songs are inspired by less known authors or other artists. For instance, another work of art is Si Tú, Si Yo, Si No. This song was first written and published by one of his points of reference Kiko Veneno. If you want to compare the two versions, here are two links to listen to the original version by Veneno and Jairo's later reinterpretation.

Another reinterpretation is inspired by one of Josele Sanguesa's creations Soy Luz. Soy Luz is featured in his disc Visto lo Visto. Among some of Sanguesa's most appreciated works, I would love to signal Playa. Guess what? Our beloved Jairo knows when a song is great. He just knows. Which is how Playa entered his repertory as well. Both versions are absolutely beautiful. Take a look at the original version here. Jairo's version can be found here instead (min 2.22).

One more song that probably not so many people konw being a version is La Quiero a Morir. Jairo's French followers can surely make the instant connection with Francis Cabrel's successJe l'aime à mourir. As done before, here are two links to help you appreciate both the "original song and Jairo's reinterpretation.
